The History of computers

In this presentation we will explore the computers' history

Early Mechanical Computers

Abacus(Circa 500BC)

was used by ancient civilizations
for basic arithmetic.

anatycal-engine

It was designed to be programmable using punch cards

Astrolab

an ancient astronomical instrument used to solve problems related to time and the position of celestial objects, such as stars and planets

The Advent of Electronical computers

Moving on to the 20th century, we see the advent of electronic computers.

Alan Turing

a British mathematician, is considered one of the fathers of computer science

Eniac(1945)

ENIAC, was one of the first general-purpose electronic computers. It was designed to calculate artillery trajectories and was programmable.

Univac(1951)

The UNIVAC I (Universal Automatic Computer)
was the first commercial computer produced in the United States

The Era of Mainframes and Minicomputers :

IBM System/360 (1964)

IBM's System/360 was a revolutionary family of mainframe
computers that could run the same software
across different models, introducing the concept of compatibility.

DEC PDP-8 (1965):

The Digital Equipment Corporation's PDP-8 was the first successful minicomputer, making computing more accessible to smaller businesses and laboratories.

The personnal computers revolution

The 1970s and 1980s brought the personal computer revolution

Altair 8800 (1975)

The Altair 8800 is considered the first commercially successful personal computer,
sparking the interest of hobbyists and
leading to the creation of software like Microsoft's BASIC.

Apple II (1977)

Apple Inc. introduced the Apple II, one of the first highly successful mass-produced microcomputers. It popularized computing in homes and schools.

IBM PC (1981)

The IBM PC set the standard for personal computers and led to the widespread adoption of PCs in business and personal use.

The Rise of the Internet and Modern Computers

In the late 20th and early 21st centuries, the internet and modern computers transformed our world.

Tim Berners-Lee (1989)

Tim Berners-Lee, a British scientist, invented the World Wide Web, revolutionizing how we access and share information.

Linus Torvalds (1991)

Linus Torvalds created Linux, an open-source operating system that has become crucial for servers, supercomputers, and embedded systems.

Smartphones and Tablets (2000s)

The development of smartphones and tablets brought computing power to our fingertips, making technology more portable and integrated into our daily lives.
